ECO 203: Microeconomics for Business (BBM)

Course Info

Overview

Credits: 3   |   Lecture Hours: 48

Course Objective: Develop students’ understanding of microeconomic concepts and theories to analyze business opportunities, markets and efficient use of resources.

Key Learning Outcomes:

  • Explain basic microeconomic concepts, principles and their business relevance.
  • Use demand and supply to analyze markets, government policies, and efficiency.
  • Apply elasticity and consumer behavior to business pricing and sales decisions.
  • Analyze production, cost and pricing under different market structures.
  • Understand wage, interest, rent and profit in resource markets.

Unit-wise Syllabus (Summary)

Unit 1: Fundamentals of Microeconomics (5 LHs)

  • Microeconomics: concept and scope; microeconomics in business decisions.
  • Basic principles of economics: how people make decisions, interact, and how the economy as a whole works.

Unit 2: Analysis of Demand, Supply and Market Efficiency (12 LHs)

  • Demand and supply functions; movements vs shifts; market equilibrium.
  • Effects of taxes, subsidies, price controls on equilibrium.
  • Market efficiency: consumer & producer surplus; elasticity of demand and supply (price, income, cross, advertisement).

Unit 3: Theory of Consumer’s Behavior (4 LHs)

  • Cardinal approach: utility, MU, consumer equilibrium, demand curve.
  • Ordinal approach: indifference curves, MRS, budget line, consumer equilibrium, price effect and demand for normal goods.

Unit 4: Production and Cost Analysis (11 LHs)

  • Production function; optimal employment of inputs; isoquants & isocost; returns to scale; Cobb–Douglas function.
  • Cost concepts; short-run and long-run cost curves; economies and diseconomies of scale; AC–MC relationship.

Unit 5: Pricing Theory and Practices (10 LHs)

  • Market structure concepts and characteristics; profit maximization goal.
  • Price and output under perfect competition, monopoly, monopolistic competition.
  • Pricing practices: price discrimination, cost-plus, incremental cost pricing, two-part tariffs.

Unit 6: Markets for Resources (6 LHs)

  • Wage determination; union models; minimum wage; wage differentials; pay for performance.
  • Interest rate differentials; economic rent; business vs economic profit.
